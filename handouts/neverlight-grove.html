Neverlight Grove - Facts from Stool

Below is the information gained from Stool during your journey toward Neverlight Grove.

General Info

Roughly 150 myconids and their spore servants live here. They are divided into six circles, each ruled by a larger specimen called a circle leader; the largest specimens,called Sovereigns, govern the entire colony.

Circles

Circle of Hunters. Loobamub leads this circle. They are repsonsible for collecting corpses from the fungal wilds and returning them to the circle of Sporers for convertion into spore servants.

Circle of Builders. Gasbide leads this circle which is charged with shaping and cultivating the giant mushrooms which serve as gathering places for the myconids.

Circle of Growers. Hebopbe leads this circle which ensures the soil in the pools at the center of the grove is kept fertile, as well as tending fruiting fungi growing in the around the central portion of the grove.

Circle of Sporers. Yrberop leads this circle responsible for seeding new myconids and spore servants.

Circle of Explorers. Rasharoo runs this circle who patrol and explore the caves surrounding the grove.

Inner Circle. Brelup, Posbara, and Breberil, along with the two sovereigns form the council for this circle which governs the colony.

Sovereigns

Two soverigns manage the colony, sharing power and responsiblity.

Phylo. The elder of the two soverigns, Phylo is majestic and philosphical.

Basidia. Came to the grove shortly before Stool left. Basidia arrived with a small nomadic colony of myconids and was invited to stay and rule alongside Pyhlo for as long as it liked. Basidia's example inspired Stool to venture beyond the bounds of the grove to learn about the outside world.

Geography

The grove lies at the intersection of several fungal corridors, in a large cavern. All surfaces of the cavern are covered with fungi, including the ceiing.

Underground Marsh. Water trickles into the cavern from countless sources forming terraces, pools, and streams. This creates an undergound marsh ideal for the growth of fungi and the myconids.

Abundant Fungi.Mushrooms, molds, and lichen grow in abundance in the grove creating a bizzare and beutiful tapestry of color.

Natural Silence.Myconids do not speak, the only sounds in the grove are those of water, softened by the countelss absorbent surfaces.

Otherworldy Light. Luminescen ichen grows across the cavern and on many of the larger mushrooms, bathing the grove is soft hues of yellow, blue, and violet. Dim light suffuses the entire grove, lending it a dreamlike quality.

The Cycle. Each cycle of a myconid "day" consists of working, resting, and melding. Each of these periods takes 1/3 of the 24 hour cycle, after which the cycle repeats. Melding occurs as the myconid enters communual trance where the individual conciousness of the myconids merge to share their experiences and desires.

Spore Servants. Non-plant or fungal corpses can be sown with a special spore that overtakes the corpse's deteriorating nervous system. After a period of colonization the body is able to move and respond to commands from myconids, but retains no memories of it's past life. Stool assures you the grove only uses these spores on corpses they gather from the lands surrounding the grove.
