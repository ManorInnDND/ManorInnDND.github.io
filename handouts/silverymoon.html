Introduction TLDR

Silverymoon looks more like a series of gardens or forest glades than a stone city, and thanks to all the growing things, it reeks less than most settlements a third its size. It's also quieter, thanks tothe gentle breezes and sound-muting properties of the protective wards, which also protect the city from extremes of weather. A series of dwarf-built cisterns, pumps and piping ensure the city has flushed privies and ample fresh water for drinking and gardening.

The lush beauty of the city and its many forest districts make it easy for newcomers to become lost. Thankfully, Silvaeren don't mind offering directions, and every cistern cover in the cobbles of an intersection has an arrow graven into it, pointing north.

Most city buildings have cellars, and four or even five floors above them, but the trees and gently rolling landscaping make the spires of Silverymoon less imposing than similar size structures in other cities. The older path of the city is Northbank, and the newer, still expanding area is Southbank. A magical bridge of force spans the river between these parts, called... the Moonbridge. Four gates lead out of the city: Moorgate leads northwest, Hunter's Gate north (into the Moonwood), Sundabar gate to the east, and New Gate to the south.

In Silverymoon knowledge is highly valued. Most folk find satisfaction in being well informed in at least one field, and Silvaeren love witty sayings, sharing jokes, lore, music, readings of ballads, poems, and romantic fiction; most attend revels or private dances and feasts three days of the week. They tend to dabble in many interests so shops alternate often, but the small, cozy botuiqes are always crammed with beautiful, fascinating objects, small magics, books, and maps.

Unquestionably the foremost center of learning and culture in the North, Silverymoon is a happy place where people of many races (surface good-aligned humanoids) dwell in peace. Much of the feeling of peace and goodwill is due to the influence of powerful local mages and the Harpers. Both forces are joined in the lady who shaped modern Silverymoon, Alustriel, known to Silvaeren as Lady Hope.

Alustriel prefers to keep people happy and hopeful thorugh enlightened rule, but recognizes intrigue and evil deeds are unaviodable and has an efficient cadre of spies and agents to guard against such influences. The city's army, the Knights in Silver, patrol the lands 7 day's ride outside the city to the south and east.

General Info

General Features

The city of Silverymoon traces it's history back over 700 years. It is the second largest city of the north, after Waterdeep.

Naturalist Architecture. Though bustling, the city's architects have adhered to a sustainable ethos throughout its expansion. Towering oaks, slender spires, and mage-sculpted stone buildings crowd the city's avenues and most of the flagstone streets are shaded by blueleaf elms. Many of the older buildings are clad in a thin layer of fused royal blue or emerald green glass. Balconies and curving stairs are everywhere, with windowsills, railings, and eaves adorned with herbs and flowers suspended in sculpted bowls.

Magical Wards The city is protected by ovular wards extending several hundred feet beyond it's outer walls. The wards emit a faint, predominantly pink, irridescence. The extent of the ward's effects are secret but they are known to protect the city from extremes in weather and prevent evocation and teleportation magic. It is rumored that evil hearts cannot pass beyond them, though the city's bloody history suggests otherwise.

Sound.The wards dampen sound above a certain pitch, even the busiest streets are pleasingly muted.

Personages

The city boasts hundreds of notables, the most noteworth listed below.

Lady Alustriel. Most prominent being in the city, former high mage rumored to be hundreds of years old. Wise, kind, and a judicious ruler, her reign has secured Silvermoon as a haven of harmony and collaborative effort. Appears as a middle aged human woman with striking white hair.

Taern 'Thunderspell' Hornblade. Current High Mage, leader of the city. Former leader of the Spellgaurd, a group of mages in service of the cities defense. Older human male with cropped hair and beard, slow to anger.

Jorus Azurmantel. Leader of Spellgaurd. Middle aged half-elven jokester.

Sernius Alathar. Leader of the Knights in Silver. The city's army of elite warriors.

Others. There are hundreds of dignitaries, warriors, wizards, and sages in Silverymoon.

Locales

Several of the more prominent places are listed below.

Civic Centers. The High Palace, protected by an additional wards of powerful but uncertain function. White stone towers and battlements appear carved from a single block, the battlements boast rearing unicorn merlons in place of more traditional rectangles. The Star Court, central building for courts and assemblies, register here if you want to be a citizen.

Schools. Arkens Invocatorium, run by Arken the Icy. School dedicated to evocation research and proficiency. The Lady's College, school of magical sponsered by Alustriel, tution may be paid by service in Spellguard. Teaches music as well, reknown for producing many famous harpers. Utrumm's Conservatory, famed school of music.

Libraries. Silverymoon is famed for it's respect for lore. The Map House contains maps of the entire north as well as geneaologies of many Silvarean families. The Vault of Sages is a reknown storehouse of knowlege, a five-story horseshoe shaped monolith. Librarians spend 10 years learning the system of bookkeeping for the vaults under the building.

Inns. Dozens of inns, more famous are: The Bright Blade Brandished, an adventures inn where everyone is welcome and no brawling is permitted. The Golden Oak, expensive inn built around massive oak tree, the cook you rescued worked here. The Dancing Goat, bawdry place with escorts that never closes. The Hammer and the Helm, only dwarves are welcome here, but comely maids and 'sagh dawi' (trusted fighting companions) are allowed in. Proudfoot Tavern, typical mid-sized tavern, noted for hosting Dragon Chess championship.

Shops. The more notable are: Bluebottle, extensive selection of wines. Dornsar Leathers, best leather in town, elven made, exotic leather masks. Optym's Blade, armorer and weapons shop; owned by expert knife-thrower. Shining Scroll, magical wares. Theldymir's Crystal, glasswares, crystal balls, lenses. Wings of Amaranth, bird-being tailor, makes flashy noble and adventurer apparel with inventions sewn in. Scarlet Canvas, ship sail canvas shop, make rugged outfits for adventurers. Finklsip Finery, gnome tailor, puts feathers on everything.

Temples. Dawndancer House, Sune, goddess of passion and beauty. Everdusk Hall, Elven pantheon. Fortune Halle, Tymora, goddess of luck. Halls of Inspiration, Oghma and Milil, gods of lore and performance; largest temple. The House Invincible, Helm, god of duty, old garrison. Mielikki's Glade, Mielikki, goddess of forest and trackers. Rhyster's Matins, Lathander, morning, birth, general goodness (Aristocrat Jesus). Temple of Silver Stars, Selune, moon goddess. Tower of Balance, Mystra, goddess of magic.

TLDR

Silverymoon is a happy, good-aligned city of humans, elves, and dwarves who value knowledge, revelry and nature. It is the largest city your group has entered together with countless oppurtunies for adventure.
