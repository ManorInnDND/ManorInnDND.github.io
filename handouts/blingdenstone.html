Blingdenstone - Facts from Baldini

Below is the information gained from Baldini during your journey toward Blingdenstone.

General Info

General Features

The town is built within the ruins, many areas still dangerous, and has some quirks unique to towns of deep gnomes.

Chambers. Blingdenstone is not a single cavern, but a network of interconnected caves and pockets the deep gnomes have widened and worked into homes, workshops, vending stalls, storerooms, and the like. Other than residential caverns, all chambers are tall enough for medium creatures to walk about in without trouble.

Many Stairs. Larger caverns are rarely bare, with stone stairs and bridges crisscrossing creating access to tunnels high above the cavern floor.

Light.The caverns of the inner town are brightly lit with a mix of bioluminscent fungi, caged giant fire beetles, and magic. The light from these chambers spills into short connecting tunnels lacking their own illumination creating areas of dim light. Longer tunnels have their own light sources.

Connecting Tunnels. Deep gnomes and other small races can walk without difficulty, but larger creatures must duck and squeeze- a feature intended to control invaders' movements. Most tunnels have smooth floors but those near the edges of the reclaimed areas are strewn with rubble and dust. Many tunnels incorporate traps such as collapsing ceilings or floor, or allow for flooding, but the traps are not currently active.

Doors. Most doors in the town are finely constructed, with adamantine and steel inlays making them incredibly resiliant to forced entry.

About 300 deep gnomes inhabit the ruins of the once-secret town of Blingdenstone, which was destroyed by a drow invasion 50 years ago. In the last decade deep gnomes have returned to the ruins to resettle and established themselves in the residences near the town's main entrance. The rest of the town's neighboorhoods are sealed off, deemed uninhabitable due to varied dangers.

Personages

A few prominent deep gnomes take an interest in the party and may have tasks for them.

Dorbo Diggermattock. Dorbo is king of the deep gnomes and is in charge of the mining operations in the surrouding areas. He is particularly lean for a deep gnome and has a piercing gaze.

Senni Diggermattock. Senni is queen of Blingdenstone and is in charge of the political and general social affairs of the town and its settlers. She is also lean, like her husband, and is likewise stern.

Kazook Pickshine. Short and pudgy, Kazook is an esteemed merchant in Blingdenstone, with contracts among the duerger of Gracklestugh and the shield dwarves of Mithral Hall. Like all deep gnomes he deals in gems, but also trades exotic ores and herbs. He is a distinguised alchemist as well. Kazook is the only male merchant in Blingdenstone, his time exploring outside the town having broadened his mind.

Barrow Warden Jadger. Jadger is the ghost of a deep gnome veteran who died defending the town during the drow assault 50 years ago. He presents an unpleasant aspect as his head is split nearly in two, and one half of his face droops with palsy.

Glyphic Shroombright. Glyphic is the solicitous head priest of the temple to Callurdan Stonehands in Blingdenstone. He fidgets often and seems obssesed with candles, specifically ensuring that they are burning down evenly with respect to their neighbors.

Locales

Several of the caverns have features of interest to an adventuring group.

Traders Grotto. After visiting the Blaze Bazaar in Gracklestugh this place seems megre. However the five merchant stalls here provide a variety of goods, most imported from without the town and marked up 10-25%. The merchants are all female with the exception of Kazook.

The Ruby in the Rough. The large chamber is a holy site for the deep gnomes and is a temple dedicated to Segojan Earthcaller, the svirfneblin god of nature and deep earth. The temple is run by Glyphic Shroombright. A large passage on one of its walls leads to the vast catacombs which house the dead of Blingdenstone.

Outskirts

These caverns near the town may be of interest.

Whiteshell Mine. Blingdenstone's largest salt mine is half a day outside of the town. Baldini believes his blacksalt mine might one day rival Whiteshell.

Pickshine Mines. A secret area near Blingdenstone rumored to have rich veins of precious metals and caverns of gems.

Stoneheart Quarry. A faerzress saturated cavern where earth elementals are summoned to aid the settlers.
